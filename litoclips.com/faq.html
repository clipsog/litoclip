What platform should I start a campaign on?

Each platform suits different goals and content types. Here's what each is best for:

TikTok

TikTok is for PR. When people like you or what you do on TikTok, everybody likes you—it has that ripple effect. It's a great way to get known fast. Think of it as the entry door into many industries that you can open yourself. Build momentum here first and it carries over everywhere else.

Instagram

Instagram is more for brand and product placement. Once you're known, you can control your narrative on IG. When people support you on Instagram you gain way more influence—most people have accounts where they share their face and events from their lives, so a follow or a like means something different. That's why a lot of brands scout for new people to sponsor on IG. It's where you turn reach into credibility.

X (Twitter)

X is more for notoriety and influence—not over consumers, but over other brands and products in your industry. It's about showcasing your power. Depending on your industry, it might be best to start with other platforms first and come to X once you have a story to tell.

YouTube

YouTube is more for sales and redirections. You can redirect viewers to your original content, get views, link merch in the description, land sponsorships, and drive traffic where it matters. Use it to convert the audience you built elsewhere into buyers, subscribers, or supporters of your main work.
